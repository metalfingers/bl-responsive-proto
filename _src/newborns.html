ACTIVITY TOYS

Toys that spin, crinkle and squeak encourage your baby to fine-tune the much-needed skills of holding, grasping and letting go.

PLUSH TOYS

Giving babies a feel for different textures stimulates their sense of touch. Limit plush toys to playtime and never put them in the crib.

BATH TOYS

Bath toys make tub time more fun, but they also teach your tot to pour, squeeze, splash and explore.

SIDE-SNAP SHIRTS

Tops that open in front make it a snap to dress your newborn without irritating the umbilical cord.

BODYSUITS

Once the umbilical cord falls off, these baby basics will become your little one’s best bet for all-day, everyday comfort.

FOOTIES

Keep your peewee’s piggies warm without an endless search for lost socks. Bottom closures make diaper changes oh-so-much easier.

SWADDLE BLANKETS

Swaddle blankets are the ultimate baby soothers—their snug-as-a-bug fit reminds infants of the familiar feeling of the womb.

BEDDING & BATH

Doing the same bedtime routine each night signals to your little one that it’s time to wind down for sleep. Giving your baby a bath, reading and singing lullabies are common pre-Zs activities.

MONITOR

If you wake up in the middle of the night, a video monitor offers at-a-glance reassurance that your baby’s still snoozing.

NIGHTLIGHT

A low glow offers just enough light to help you find your way during middle-of-the-night feedings without overwhelming you with a glaring bulb.

BIBS

Cotton bibs sop up dribbles and can get tossed straight into the washing machine post-feeding. Firmer styles with pockets catch plummeting morsels and wipe clean with a damp cloth.

BURP CLOTHS

Spills and spit up are the new normal. Keep a stockpile of burp cloths on hand.

PLATES, BOWLS & CUPS

Sippies are a great (read: spill-proof!) way to transition your growing tot from a bottle to an open cup.

HIGH CHAIR

Always strap your child into a high chair and place it far enough away from walls and counters that your little kicker can’t accidentally tip over.

BOUNCER

The nest-like seat and rocking motion make this a comforting pick. Just be sure to secure your little me and stay within eyeshot.

CAR SEAT

Your baby’s first car seat should be designed specifically for infants and sit rear-facing in the car—the safest way for tiny tots to travel.

CARRIER

Most newborns crave close contact with their parents. A baby carrier lets you snuggle up while keeping your hands free.

LIGHTWEIGHT STROLLER + ACCESSORIES

Great for travel and running errands, lightweight strollers are easy to pick up, fold and store, but shouldn’t be used until around age 6 months when babies develop better head and neck control.

FULL-SIZE STROLLER + ACCESSORIES

Because infants can’t sit up on their own, the seat on their first set of wheels should be able to recline to flat. Look for a full-size stroller with different seating options so it grows with your child.
